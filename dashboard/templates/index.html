📊 Understanding Your Performance Metrics

Total Return
The total percentage gain or loss of your strategy over the entire period. If you started with $10,000 and ended with $12,500, your total return is +25%.
✅ What's good: Anything positive. Compare it to "Buy & Hold" to see if your strategy was worth the effort.
Annualised Return
Your total return converted to "per year", so you can fairly compare a 1-year and 5-year backtest. A 5-year test that returned 50% total = roughly 8.4% per year annualised.
✅ What's good: The S&P 500 has averaged about 10% per year historically. Beating that consistently is hard.
Sharpe Ratio
Return divided by risk (volatility). A strategy earning 20% with wild swings might score lower than one earning 12% very smoothly. It answers: "how much return did I get for each unit of risk I took?"
✅ What's good: Above 1.0 is good. Above 2.0 is excellent. Negative means you're losing money on a risk-adjusted basis. Most professional funds aim for 1.0–1.5.
Maximum Drawdown
The worst percentage drop from a peak to the lowest point before recovery. -20% means at some point your $10,000 fell to $8,000 before climbing back.
⚠️ This is the "how stressed would you feel?" number. A -50% drawdown means you'd have watched half your money disappear, even if it eventually recovered. Most people sell in panic long before recovery.
Volatility (Annualised)
How wildly the price swings day-to-day, scaled to a full year. It measures the size of the ups and downs, regardless of direction.
Reference: Cash ≈ 0%, Bonds ≈ 5%, S&P 500 ≈ 15%, Individual stocks ≈ 25-40%, Crypto ≈ 60-80%+
Strategy Grade (A–D)
A combined score based on Sharpe ratio, max drawdown, and whether the strategy beat buy & hold. A = excellent, B = good, C = average, D = poor.
A grade is just a quick summary — always look at the individual numbers too.

📈 Understanding the Chart

Candlestick Chart (top panel)
Each candle shows one day of trading. Green candle = price went up that day. Red candle = price went down. The thin lines (wicks) show the highest and lowest price reached during the day.
Moving Average Lines (SMA)
A moving average smooths out price noise by averaging the last N days. The yellow line is the short (fast) average; the blue line is the long (slow) average.
🟡 Golden Cross: yellow crosses ABOVE blue → BUY signal. 📉 Death Cross: yellow crosses BELOW blue → SELL signal.
Bollinger Bands (faint blue channel)
A price channel showing "normal" range based on recent volatility. When price touches the upper band, it may be stretched upward. When it touches the lower band, it may be stretched downward.
Cumulative Returns (middle panel)
Purple line = your strategy's total gain/loss over time. Dotted grey line = "Buy & Hold" (just buying on day 1 and doing nothing). When purple is above grey, your strategy is winning.
RSI — Relative Strength Index (bottom panel)
A number between 0–100 measuring how "tired" or "stretched" recent price movement is. Think of it like a rubber band — the further it stretches, the more likely it snaps back.
🔴 Above 70 = Overbought (been going up a lot, may cool off). 🟢 Below 30 = Oversold (been going down a lot, may bounce). ⚪ 30-70 = Neutral zone.

💡 The Moving Average Crossover Strategy

This is one of the oldest and most studied strategies in finance. The rules are simple:

  • Buy when the short (fast) MA crosses above the long (slow) MA — called a "Golden Cross". Signals that short-term momentum is picking up above the long-term trend.
  • Sell when the short MA crosses back below the long MA — called a "Death Cross". Signals that momentum is fading.
  • While you hold a "buy" position, your returns follow the stock. When you're in a "sell" position, you hold cash (earning 0%).

Important: It often underperforms simple buy & hold in bull markets because it gets you out during dips that later recover. Where it shines is in avoiding major crashes.

✅ What Makes a Good Backtest?

  • ✅Sharpe ratio above 1.0
  • ✅Max drawdown smaller (less negative) than buy & hold
  • ✅Tested over at least 5 years of data
  • ✅Reasonable number of trades (not hundreds — each costs fees)
  • ⚠️Even if it underperforms buy & hold in returns, if it does so with MUCH less drawdown, it might be worth it psychologically
  • ❌Do not rely on a strategy that only worked for one short time period

💼 Using the Portfolio Tab

The Portfolio tab lets you track a "paper" (fake) portfolio. Add any stock with the number of shares and the price you pretended to pay. The dashboard will fetch live prices and show you your total value, profit/loss per position, and a plain-English summary.

This is the same as what Trading 212 shows you, just without real money changing hands.

🔍 Using the Optimizer Tab

The Optimizer tests 16 different combinations of short and long moving average windows on the same stock and time period, then ranks them by Sharpe ratio so you can find the best settings for that specific market.

Warning: Settings that worked well in a past backtest don't always work in the future. This is called "overfitting". Use the optimizer to understand the range of possible outcomes, not as a prediction.
